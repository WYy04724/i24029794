Welcome to Qingdao

Qingdao, a picturesque coastal city located on the eastern coast of China, is one of the country's most beloved destinations. Known for its stunning seascape, rich cultural heritage, and world-famous Tsingtao Beer, Qingdao seamlessly blends modern urban life with natural beauty.

From the iconic red tiles and green trees of the old town to the gleaming skyscrapers of the new district, from the emerald waters of Laoshan Mountain to the golden sands of its beaches, Qingdao offers a unique experience that captivates every visitor.

From German colonial architecture to ancient Chinese temples, Qingdao's history spans thousands of years with diverse cultural influences.

World-Class Cuisine

Fresh seafood caught daily, legendary street food on Pichai Courtyard, and the internationally renowned Tsingtao Beer.

Stunning Nature

Mount Lao's misty peaks, crystal-clear bays, and miles of pristine coastline create a paradise for nature lovers.

Local Tips

The best time to visit Qingdao is from June to October. Summer (July-August) is perfect for beach activities and the famous Qingdao International Beer Festival. Spring (April-May) offers mild weather and blooming flowers in Badaguan. Autumn (September-October) is ideal for hiking Mount Lao with clear skies and comfortable temperatures.

Qingdao has an extensive metro system with 6 lines covering major attractions. Taxis are affordable and readily available. Ride-hailing apps like Didi are widely used. For exploring the old town, walking is the best option. Buses cover areas not reached by the metro. Car rental is available for trips to Mount Lao and surrounding areas.

For first-time visitors, stay near the old town (Shinan District) to be close to Zhanqiao Pier, Catholic Church, and Badaguan. The Shinan area offers a mix of boutique hotels and international chains. For a modern experience, the May Fourth Square area has luxury hotels with ocean views. Budget travelers can find excellent hostels near the university district.

Try draft Tsingtao Beer from a plastic bag — it's a local tradition! Visit Pichai Courtyard after 7 PM for the best street food experience. Bring comfortable walking shoes for exploring the old town's hilly streets. Learn a few basic Mandarin phrases as English is not widely spoken. Download offline maps before arriving as some areas have limited signal.
