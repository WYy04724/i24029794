Iconic Landmarks

The must-see attractions that define Qingdao

Zhanqiao Pier

The most famous landmark of Qingdao, this 440-meter-long pier extends into Qingdao Bay. Built in 1892, its octagonal Huilan Pavilion at the tip has appeared on countless postcards and is considered the symbol of the city.

Mount Lao (Laoshan)

Known as the "most famous mountain on the sea," Laoshan rises 1,133 meters above the coastline. It is one of the birthplaces of Taoism and offers breathtaking views of mountains meeting the Yellow Sea.

Badaguan Scenic Area

Eight historic avenues lined with hundreds of villas in over 20 architectural styles including Russian, German, Japanese, and Greek. Each street is planted with a different flowering tree, creating seasonal color explosions.

More to Explore

Hidden gems and beloved local favorites

Golden Sand Beach

Stretching over 3,500 meters along the Yellow Sea, this is one of the largest and finest beaches in northern China. The golden sand, gentle waves, and well-maintained facilities make it the perfect summer destination.

Beach & Relaxation

Olympic Sailing Center

Built for the 2008 Beijing Olympics sailing events, this world-class marina has become a symbol of Qingdao's identity as the "Sailing City." The stunning architecture and harbor views are mesmerizing at sunset.

Modern Landmark

St. Michael's Cathedral

A magnificent Gothic-Romanesque cathedral built in 1934, featuring twin 56-meter-high spires. It is the largest Catholic church in Qingdao and a beloved photography spot, especially during golden hour.

Architecture

May Fourth Square

The heart of modern Qingdao, famous for its iconic "Wind of May" sculpture in vibrant red. The square overlooks Fushan Bay and offers spectacular views of the Olympic Sailing Center across the water.

City Center

Signal Hill Park

The best panoramic viewpoint in Qingdao, offering a 360-degree vista of the red-roofed old town, the blue bay, and distant mountains. The three red mushroom-shaped observation domes at the summit are iconic.

Panoramic View

Xiaoyushan Park

A hidden gem overlooking the old town from the east. The traditional Chinese-style pavilions provide the perfect frame for photographing Qingdao's famous "red tiles, green trees, blue sky, and clear sea" panorama.

Hidden Gem
